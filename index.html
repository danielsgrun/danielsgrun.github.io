About Me

Who am I?

My name is Daniel, I am a Masters student in Physics, and I am currently devoted to learn how to enhance the development of quantum devices. In particular, my Masters goal is to investigate how the use of integrability, once a subject of interest only to mathematicians, could increase the quantum dynamics coherence of a given system. I hope to increasingly contribute to the AMO Physics through small and concatenated advances on the so-called New Quantum Revolution.




What do I do?

Right now, I am working in the Institute for Experimental Physics at the University of Innsbruck. Interested by the experimental work that is being done on the field of AMO Physics, I have recently joined Prof. Francesca Ferlaino's Erbium Group under the Excellence Fellowship for Visiting Masters Students. Having dealt with the basics of lens alignment, AOM functioning and the electronics of PID - using the latter two for the stabilization of laser's intensity -, I will now start to use a DMD (Digital Micromirror Device), with the goal of reproducing a Bragg Spectroscopy setup (and then enhance it).

Before, I had been working since 2018 at the Mathematical Physics and Integrable Systems Group, headed by Prof. Angela Foerster, studying the integrability of a recently introduced family of models that describe quantum tunneling between Bose-Einstein Condensates, which was the main subject of my bachelor thesis.

In our first paper, we demonstrated the existence of a Heisenberg-sensitive interferometer in the integrable four-well model. This model can be obtained through an extension of que Quantum Inverse Scattering Method, although it may also derive from the Extended Bose-Hubbard Model (which includes long-range interactions) through a suitable choice of parameters. We showed that it works as a controlled-phase gate if the Fock states are mapped into two-qudit states, which are the immediate analogy to "qubits with higher Hilbert space dimension".

After that, we came up with two protocols for NOON state generation and arbitrary phase-encoding on them, which should be possible by employing breaking of integrability on a controllable manner and in specific time steps. An analysis on the system's experimental feasibility was also developed. Theses results were compiled into our second paper, which should be on Arxiv soon.

Experience

Recent Work Experience

Atomtronic quantum devices 2019/1 - Present

The special properties of integrable models allow for enhanced quantum devices to be built. In special, we have shown that, in the interable limit of an Extended Bose-Hubbard model for a four-well configuration, the system acts as both a Heisenberg-sensitive interferometer to detect NOON states and as a NOON state blackbox processor itself. This seems to prove integrable models posess enhanced properties related to quantum dynamics and device applications.

Physical design of integrable models 2019/1 - Present

The study of thermalization processes in physical systems is a very special subject. Among other things, this is due to the necessity of developing a qubit (or, in our case, qudit) platform whose dynamics is easily controlled and manifests coherence, being able to perform high quality logical operations with less thermalization loss. In this context, it is desirable to experimentally realize integrable model (in particular, the four-well integrable model), since they usually do not present chaotic behaviour.

Integrability and quantum tunneling 2018/1 - 2018/2

After deciding to move from Observational Astrophysics to Theoretical Physics, I started to work with Prof. Angela Foerster in her Mathematical Physics and Integrable Systems Research Group. There, I learned a lot about Yang-Baxter Algebra, quantum dynamics on tunneling multi-well models and conserved charges (in the context of integrable systems).
